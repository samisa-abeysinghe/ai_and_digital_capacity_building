Standards & Maturity Framework

A rigorous architectural approach to digital literacy, aligning rural empowerment with global benchmarks and localized livelihood outcomes.

Understanding the Standards

This project uses a "Multi-Framework" approach to ensure both technical accuracy and social impact:

  • UNESCO DLGF (Digital Literacy Global Framework): Based on the European DigComp 2.1, it defines the technical competence areas required to thrive in a digital society.
  • UNICEF DQ (Digital Intelligence): Focuses on the socio-emotional and ethical dimensions of digital use, particularly for younger personas and family safety.
  • Sri Lankan Livelihood Injector: A custom local layer that maps global competencies to specific regional utilities (LankaQR, Gov e-Services, Helakuru).

Maturity Level Guide (M0 - M3)

How we measure progress from awareness to community leadership.

M0: Awareness (Fearful to Familiar)

Learner: "I know this exists but I am afraid to touch it."

Trainer: Focus on demystification and building basic physical confidence with the device.

M1: Basic Use (Guided Action)

Learner: "I can perform a task if someone shows me exactly where to click."

Trainer: Focus on repetition and "See one, Do one" pedagogical methods.

M2: Independent Use (Self-Sufficient)

Learner: "I can use the app safely on my own and verify official sources."

Trainer: Focus on "Verify Before Trust" (VBT) and error-handling without help.

M3: Applied Outcomes (Livelihood + Mentorship)

Learner: "I use this tool to save time/money, and I can teach my neighbor."

Trainer: Focus on the Livelihood Challenge and the Help-a-Neighbor protocol.

🏅 The "M3" Certification Rule

In this framework, **Certification** is only granted when a participant successfully demonstrates the M3 "Applied Outcome" for their persona. Attendance is not enough; practical impact is the only measure of success.
