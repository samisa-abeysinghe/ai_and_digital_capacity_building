CORE-03: Safety, Trust & Privacy

The "Hard Gate" for digital empowerment. Learn the Verify Before Trust (VBT) rules specifically for the Sri Lankan digital landscape.

Maturity Entry: M0 Awareness Maturity Target: M3 Applied Outcomes UNESCO Alignment: Safety (4.1/4.2) Session: S3 (45-60m)

1. Concept: Verify Before Trust (VBT)

Digital safety in Sri Lanka is about recognizing social engineering. Scammers often use the name of your bank or the government to create a sense of urgency.

  • Official IDs: Checking the "Sender Name" on SMS (e.g., SL-GOVT vs. a random 10-digit number).
  • Link Verification: Looking for ".gov.lk" or official banking domains.
  • Intent Check: Why is this person asking for a code I just received on my phone?

🛑 The "Golden Rules" of No-Share

In Sri Lanka, **no official** (Bank, GN, or Police) will ever ask for these via a phone call or WhatsApp:

  • OTP (One Time Password): The 6-digit code for transactions or login.
  • ATM PIN: Your secret 4-digit card number.
  • Mobile Banking Password: Even if they claim "your account is blocked."

🛠 Lab Activity: Spotting the local "WhatsApp OTP Scam"

Analyze the scenario provided on the DigiTruck screen:

"A friend's account is hacked. They message you: 'I accidentally sent my code to your phone, can you send it back to me?'"

  • Task 1: Identify the red flag (Real codes are never 'sent by accident').
  • Task 2: Practice the "Report & Block" flow on a dummy WhatsApp interface.
  • Task 3: Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) on your own device.

🏆 M3 Livelihood Challenge

Task: Audit your own phone's privacy settings. You must set your WhatsApp "Last Seen" to 'My Contacts' and ensure your screen lock (PIN/Fingerprint) is active. Demonstrate to the trainer that you can "Freeze" a dummy bank card in under 30 seconds.

🤝 The "Help-a-Neighbor" Protocol

M3 Mastery means being the shield for your village. Your task:

"Show one family member how to enable 2FA on their account. Explain that the OTP is like the key to their house—never give it to a stranger."
