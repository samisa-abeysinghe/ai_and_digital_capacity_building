CORE-03: Safety, Trust & Privacy

The "Hard Gate" for digital empowerment. Learn to build trust and verify before acting in the digital world.

Maturity Entry: M0 Awareness Maturity Target: M1-M2 Basic/Independent UNESCO Alignment: Safety (4.1/4.2) Session: S3 (45-60m)

1. Concept: Verify Before Trust (VBT)

Trust in the digital world is not given; it is earned through verification. In this module, we move from being "Digitally Fearful" to "Digitally Alert."

  • Verify the Source: Is this really your bank or the government?
  • Verify the Link: Is the URL spelled correctly?
  • Verify the Intent: Why is this message asking for money or an OTP right now?

🛑 The "Never Share" Rule

Regardless of who asks, NEVER share these items over a call, message, or email:

  • OTP (One Time Password)
  • ATM Pin / Banking PIN
  • Account Passwords
  • Full Identity Details (unverified)

Real entities will never ask for these via insecure channels.

🛠 Lab Activity: The "Spot the Fake" Test

Look at two sample messages on the DigiTruck screen. Use the VBT rules to identify which one is a "Scam" and which one is "Official."

  • Step 1: Check the sender's phone number or ID.
  • Step 2: Look for grammatical errors or "urgent" language.
  • Step 3: Check if the link goes to a ".gov.lk" or official domain.

2. Digital Privacy Basics

Privacy is about control. You decide who sees your information and how it is used.

  • Social Media Privacy: Who can see your photos?
  • App Permissions: Why does a calculator app need your location?
  • Data Minimization: Only share what is strictly necessary.

📄 Job Aid: 1-Minute Safety Checklist

Before you click "OK" or "Send," ask yourself:

  1. Did I expect this message/call?
  2. Does this message make me feel urgent or scared?
  3. Have I verified the identity of the person?
  4. Am I being asked for an OTP or PIN? (If yes, STOP).

Trust is Built, Verification is Constant.
