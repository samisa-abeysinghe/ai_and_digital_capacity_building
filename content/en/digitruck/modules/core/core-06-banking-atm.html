CORE-06: Digital Banking & ATM Use

Bridge the gap between physical cash and digital finance safely. Master ATM Security and mobile banking for family and business.

Maturity Entry: M1 Basic Use Maturity Target: M3 Applied Outcomes UNESCO Alignment: Problem Solving (5.2) Session: S5 (60-75m)

1. Concept: The "Physical-to-Digital" Bridge

In rural Sri Lanka, the ATM is your primary link to the digital economy. Managing it safely is the foundation of financial inclusion.

  • ATM Shielding: Protecting your PIN from cameras and "shoulder surfers."
  • SMS Alert Discipline: Ensuring your current mobile number is registered for instant transaction notifications.
  • Balance Transparency: Using your phone to check "Pension" or "Salary" credits without visiting the bank.

🛡 The "No Help from Strangers" Protocol

If the ATM machine is "slow" or your card gets stuck, **never** accept help from a bystander. Walk into the bank or call the official 24/7 hotline immediately. Scammers often linger near ATMs to "help" and swap cards.

🛠 Lab Activity: The Mobile Banking Dashboard

Using the banking simulator, perform these M3 tasks:

  • Task 1: Navigate to "Transaction History" and identify a mock suspicious payment.
  • Task 2: Practice adding a "Beneficiary" using only their Account Number and Bank/Branch code.
  • Task 3: Locate the "E-Statement" or "Activity Log" to verify a GovPay receipt.

🏆 M3 Livelihood Challenge

Task: Plan a "Digital Payment" for a real household bill (e.g., CEB Electricity or Water Board). You must identify the correct **Reference Number** on your physical bill and demonstrate how to enter it into the banking app safely. **Save the digital receipt** to a dedicated folder on your phone.

🤝 The "Help-a-Neighbor" Protocol

M3 Mastery means reducing the fear of others. Your task:

"Show an elderly neighbor how to check their bank balance via SMS or App so they don't have to travel to the town just to see if their pension has arrived."
