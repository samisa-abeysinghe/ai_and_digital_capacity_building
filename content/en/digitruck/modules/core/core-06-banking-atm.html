CORE-06: Digital Banking & ATM Use

Bridge the gap between physical cash and digital finance. Learn to use ATMs safely and manage your money via mobile banking.

Maturity Entry: M1 Basic Use Maturity Target: M2 Independent Use UNESCO Alignment: Problem Solving (5.2) Session: S5 (60-75m)

1. Concept: Physical-to-Digital Transition

Digital banking isn't just about apps; it's about how you manage your relationship with money. The ATM is your bridge between the physical world (cash) and the digital world (your account balance).

  • ATM Safety: Protecting your physical space.
  • Balance & History: Using your phone to check what you have before you spend.
  • Digital Ticketing: Moving from paper tickets to QR-based transport and event passes.

🛡 The "ATM Shield" Protocol

When using an Automated Teller Machine (ATM), follow these professional safety rules:

  • Cover the Keypad: Use your hand to hide your PIN entry from cameras or people behind you.
  • Check for Skimmers: Gently wiggle the card slot; it should be firm, not loose or added-on.
  • No Help from Strangers: Never let a stranger "help" you with your card or PIN. If the machine eats your card, call the bank directly.

🛠 Lab Activity: The Mobile Banking Dashboard

Use the simulator to perform three high-utility tasks:

  • Task 1: Check your balance and "Transaction History" to spot unauthorized spending.
  • Task 2: Add a "Beneficiary" (someone you trust) for a quick transfer.
  • Task 3: Purchase a "Digital Ticket" for a bus or train and save the QR to your gallery.

2. Managing Alerts & Security

Your phone is your security guard. Set it up to tell you whenever money moves.

  • SMS Alerts: Ensure your current phone number is linked to your account.
  • Daily Limits: Set a maximum amount that can be spent digitally in one day.
  • Biometric Login: Use your fingerprint or face instead of a typed password for faster, safer access.

📄 Job Aid: What to do if something goes wrong?

  1. Loss of Phone/Card: Call your bank's 24/7 hotline immediately to "Freeze" your accounts.
  2. Unrecognized Transaction: Do not panic. Note the date and amount and visit your branch or call support.
  3. Forgot PIN: Never write it down. Use the "Reset PIN" feature in your official app or visit the bank.
