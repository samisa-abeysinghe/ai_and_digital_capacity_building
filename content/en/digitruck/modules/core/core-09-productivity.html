CORE-09: Problem-Solving & Productivity Habits

Work smarter, not harder. Master the digital tools and habits that help you organize your life and business within the Sri Lankan community.

Maturity Entry: M1 Basic Use Maturity Target: M3 Applied Outcomes UNESCO Alignment: Problem Solving (5.1/5.4) Session: S7 (45m)

1. Concept: The Organized Digital Life

Productivity in Sri Lanka is about moving away from "Mental Notes" to a digital system that keeps you on track with Harvest cycles, GN clinic dates, and bill payments.

  • Search as a Skill: Finding files or messages in seconds using keywords (e.g., "Electricity Bill Jan").
  • Cloud Continuity: Ensuring your most important documents (NIC scan, Birth Certificate) are safe even if your physical phone is lost.
  • Task Lists: Using checklists to ensure nothing is forgotten on "Market Day" or "Government Office Visit Day."

🛠 Lab Activity: Setting Up Your Weekly System

Practice these three M3 habits:

  • Habit 1: The Calendar. Add an appointment for "GN Follow-up" and set an SMS/Push Alert for 24 hours before.
  • Habit 2: The Digital Folder. Create a folder on your phone named "Official_Docs" and move your NIC scan into it.
  • Habit 3: The Search. Use the "Global Search" bar on your phone to find a specific contact by only searching for their "Division" or "Job Title" in your notes.

⚠️ Backup Security: Two-Factor (2FA)

If you store your life in the cloud (Google Drive / iCloud), you **must** have 2FA enabled. If you lose access to your recovery phone number, you may lose your digital documents forever. **Secure the backup first.**

🏆 M3 Livelihood Challenge

Task: Create a "Business/Household Planning" system. Set up a shared calendar event or a checklist for a recurring task (e.g., "Monthly Pension Check" or "Weekly Stock Update"). Demonstrate that you can find this list in under 5 seconds using Search.

🤝 The "Help-a-Neighbor" Protocol

M3 Mastery means reducing stress for others. Your task:

"Identify a friend who is always stressed about missing dates or losing papers. Show them how to take a clear 'Scanning Photo' of a document and save it in a named folder on their phone."
