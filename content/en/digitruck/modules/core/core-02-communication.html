CORE-02: Communication & Digital Etiquette

Professional communication for the digital age. Learn how to use messaging tools like WhatsApp and Email to build trust and grow your reputation.

Maturity Entry: M0 Awareness Maturity Target: M3 Applied Outcomes UNESCO Alignment: Communication (3.1) Session: S2 (45m)

1. Concept: Respectful Official Communication

In Sri Lanka, professional digital interaction follows specific norms. Your messages represent your integrity and the quality of your work.

  • Official Tone: Using respectful greetings (e.g., "Good morning, Officer/Sir/Madam") in WhatsApp and Email.
  • Group Etiquette: Avoiding "Spam" and unverified forwards in professional community groups.
  • Language Clarity: Using clear Sinhala/Tamil/English to ensure your request is understood by DS or GN officials.

🛠 Lab Activity: The Professional Inquiry Flow

Practice drafting an official message for a service request:

  • Step 1: Open WhatsApp or Email.
  • Step 2: Draft a 3-sentence message: Greet respectfully, state your request clearly (e.g., "Requesting status of my business registration"), and thank the recipient.
  • Step 3: Use the "Voice Input" feature in Helakuru to draft the message hands-free.

🛑 Ethics Alert: The "Forward" Rule

Before forwarding any news or "health tip" to a group: **Verify the source.** If it does not come from an official government or news channel, do not share it. Misinformation can cause real-world harm in your community.

🏆 M3 Livelihood Challenge

Task: Draft a professional WhatsApp inquiry for a potential supplier or customer. You must include a "Clear Call to Action" (e.g., "Please let me know the price for 10 units") and use correct spacing and punctuation. Send this to your "Digital Buddy" for verification.

🤝 The "Help-a-Neighbor" Protocol

M3 Mastery means improving your local network. Your task:

"Identify a neighbor who frequently sends 'Spam' or chain-messages. Gently explain how to check if a message is a 'Rumour' or 'Official News' before they hit forward."
