CORE-02: Communication & Digital Etiquette

Professional communication for the digital age. Learn how to use messaging and social tools effectively while maintaining professional boundaries.

Maturity Entry: M0 Awareness Maturity Target: M1-M2 Basic/Independent UNESCO Alignment: Communication (3.1) Session: S2 (45m)

1. Concept: Professional Messaging

Digital communication is more than just "chatting." In a professional or official context, your messages represent your personal and business brand.

  • Clarity & Brevity: Getting to the point quickly and respectfully.
  • Tone: Using appropriate language for the recipient (Official vs. Friend).
  • Context: Knowing when to use Voice, Text, or Video.

🛑 Digital Boundaries

Respecting others' digital space is key to building trust:

  • Timing: Avoid sending professional messages late at night or very early morning.
  • Consent: Ask before adding someone to a group or starting a video call.
  • Privacy: Never forward a private message or photo without the sender's permission.

🛠 Lab Activity: Drafting a Professional Message

Practice drafting a WhatsApp message to a government official or a city-based supplier:

  • Step 1 (The Greeting): "Dear [Name/Title], good morning."
  • Step 2 (The Purpose): "I am writing regarding [Topic/Issue]..."
  • Step 3 (The Ask): "Could you please let me know the status of [Request]?"
  • Step 4 (The Closing): "Thank you, [Your Name]."

2. Social Media Etiquette

Social media is a public space. What you post today can be seen by potential employers or partners tomorrow.

  • Fact-Checking: Think twice before sharing news that sounds "too good" or "too scary."
  • Respectful Disagreement: How to handle conflict without "trolling."
  • Public vs Private: Understanding that nothing on the internet is truly private.

📄 Job Aid: The "Think Before You Post" Rule

Before you hit "Send" or "Share," ask:

  1. Is it True? (Have I verified it?)
  2. Is it Helpful?
  3. Is it Inspiring?
  4. Is it Necessary?
  5. Is it Kind?
