CORE-07: AI Awareness

Demystifying Artificial Intelligence. Understand what AI is, how it works, and its role as a "Digital Helper."

Maturity Entry: M0 Awareness Maturity Target: M1 Basic Use UNESCO Alignment: Information Literacy (1.3) Session: S6 (45m)

1. Concept: AI as a "Pattern Recognizer"

AI is not magic or a "thinking brain." It is a powerful tool that recognizes patterns in vast amounts of data to help us finish tasks faster.

  • Generative AI: AI that creates text, images, or code (e.g., ChatGPT, Gemini).
  • Predictive AI: AI that predicts what you might want next (e.g., YouTube recommendations).
  • The "Helper" Model: Treat AI like a smart, but occasionally mistaken, intern.

🛡 The "Verify Everything" Rule

AI can "hallucinate"—it can state false facts with total confidence. Always follow these rules:

  • Never use AI-generated health or legal advice without professional verification.
  • Never input sensitive personal, financial, or government data into a public AI tool.
  • Always check the output against an official source.

🛠 Lab Activity: Seeing AI in the Real World

Identify where AI is already helping you today:

  • Language Translation: How Google Translate "guesses" the meaning of a sentence.
  • Voice Assistants: How your phone recognizes your specific voice.
  • Spam Filters: How your email knows a message is a scam before you open it.

2. Ethics, Bias & Privacy

Because AI learns from humans, it can inherit human mistakes and biases.

  • Bias: AI might give different answers based on how it was trained.
  • Deepfakes: Being aware that AI can create very realistic but "fake" videos or voices.
  • Data Privacy: Your prompts often become training data for the AI.
