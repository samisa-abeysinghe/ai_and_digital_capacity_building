CORE-08: AI Aptitude & Prompting Lab

Move from awareness to action. Master Prompt Engineering to solve real-world livelihood problems for the Sri Lankan market.

Maturity Entry: M1 Basic Use Maturity Target: M3 Applied Outcomes UNESCO Alignment: Problem Solving (5.3) Session: S6 (60m)

1. Concept: The "Task-Context-Format" Rule

To get a professional result from AI, you must provide context. Generic prompts give generic answers. Professional prompts give local results.

  • Task: What do you want? (e.g., "Write a marketing message")
  • Context: Who is it for? (e.g., "For a boutique hotel in Colombo interested in my village-grown spices")
  • Format: How should it look? (e.g., "A formal 3-sentence WhatsApp message in professional English")

🛠 Lab Activity: Solving a Local Problem

Use the AI simulator to solve this rural entrepreneur scenario:

"You produce high-quality coconut oil. You want to offer a discount for bulk purchases to a local grocery chain."

  • Step 1: Draft a prompt using the T-C-F rule.
  • Step 2: Ask the AI to "Translate this into a polite Sinhala/Tamil message that respects our cultural values."
  • Step 3: Refine the prompt to include a specific detail (e.g., "Mention that we have a quality certificate from the local DS office").

⚠️ The "Human-in-the-Loop" Rule

Never send an AI result exactly as it is generated. Always read it, edit it to match your own voice, and verify that the facts (prices, dates, names) are 100% correct. **You are the author; the AI is the helper.**

🏆 M3 Livelihood Challenge

Task: Identify a real document you need to draft (e.g., a CV for a job, a letter to the GN for a permit, or a product description for Facebook). Use AI to draft the content, **Verify** the output, and save the final version to your "Professional Folder" on your phone.

🤝 The "Help-a-Neighbor" Protocol

M3 Mastery means sharing the multiplier. Your task:

"Next time someone is struggling to write a formal letter or a job application, show them how to use AI as a 'Drafting Assistant.' Teach them the T-C-F rule so they can get better results."
