CORE-08: AI Aptitude & Prompting Lab

Move from awareness to action. Learn the professional "Prompt Engineering" skills needed to solve real-world problems using AI.

Maturity Entry: M1 Basic Use Maturity Target: M2 Independent Use UNESCO Alignment: Problem Solving (5.3) Session: S6 (60m)

1. Concept: The "Task-Context-Format" Rule

To get the best result from an AI, you must give it clear instructions. Think of it as writing a very specific request to a smart assistant.

  • Task: What do you want the AI to do? (e.g., "Write a letter")
  • Context: Who is it for? (e.g., "To the GN officer requesting a permit")
  • Format: How should it look? (e.g., "In a formal tone, under 200 words")

🛠 Lab Activity: Solving a Livelihood Problem

Use the DigiTruck AI Lab to solve this scenario:

"You are a rural entrepreneur selling organic spices. You want to write a professional WhatsApp message to a new hotel owner in the city."

  • Step 1 (Basic Prompt): "Write a message for my spice business." (Observe the generic result).
  • Step 2 (Improved Prompt): "Write a professional 3-sentence WhatsApp message offering bulk organic cinnamon to a hotel chef. Mention I have a quality certificate." (Observe the better result).
  • Step 3 (Refinement): Ask the AI to "Make it sound more friendly but keep it professional."

⚠️ The "Human-in-the-Loop" Check

Never send or use an AI result exactly as it is. Always read it, edit it to match your voice, and verify any dates, prices, or facts it mentions. You are the responsible author, not the AI.

2. Advanced Aptitude: Summarizing & Learning

AI is an excellent tutor and researcher.

  • Summarization: "Paste this long news article and explain it in 5 bullet points for a Grade 8 student."
  • Learning: "Explain how QR codes work using an analogy about a post office."
  • Planning: "Create a 4-week study plan for me to learn basic English for business."

📄 Job Aid: 3 Golden Prompting Rules

  1. Be Specific: Use names, numbers, and clear goals.
  2. Give a Persona: "Act as a professional agriculture officer."
  3. Iterate: If the first answer is bad, ask again with more detail.
