TOT-04: Running GN Micro-Sessions & Clinics

The sustainability engine. Learn how to facilitate ongoing, small-group sessions at the Grama Niladhari level after the truck has left.

Maturity Target: M3 Applied Outcomes Focus: Long-term Continuity

1. Concept: The "Digital Clinic" Model

A Micro-Session is not a lecture; it is a "Clinic." It is a 60-minute focused session where 5-10 participants come with specific problems or to practice one specific module again.

  • Small Groups: Maximum 10 people for high interaction.
  • Single Focus: "Today we only practice QR payments" or "Today we only register for Pensions."
  • Low Resource: Can be held in a GN office, a community hall, or even under a tree—no truck needed.

🛠 Lab Activity: Planning your first GN Clinic

Design a 60-minute follow-up session for the "Women's Track":

  • Minutes 0-10: Welcome and "What has changed since the Truck day?"
  • Minutes 10-40: Hands-on Lab (e.g., Every participant must successfully scan a sample QR).
  • Minutes 40-60: Q&A and scheduling the next clinic.

⚠️ Continuity Alert

The biggest risk to the program is "Skill Fade." If a participant doesn't use the skill within 7 days of the DigiTruck visit, they will likely forget it. Micro-sessions must happen within the first 14 days of the handoff.

2. Escalation & Feedback

You are the eyes and ears of the National Task Force.

  • Documenting Success: Capturing stories of how digital tools changed a livelihood.
  • Reporting Issues: If a specific government portal or banking app is consistently failing in your division.
  • Scaling: Identifying new "Peer Trainers" from the most active participants.

📄 Job Aid: GN Clinic Toolbag

  1. A printed set of Core Module Job-Aids.
  2. One reliable smartphone with a stable data connection for demos.
  3. The GDEC attendance and progress log.
