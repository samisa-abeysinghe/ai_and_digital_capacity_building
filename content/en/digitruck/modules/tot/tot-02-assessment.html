TOT-02: Assessment & Certification Handling

Measuring impact and rewarding progress. Learn how to conduct fair, practical assessments and manage digital certifications.

Maturity Target: M3 Applied Outcomes Focus: Impact Measurement

1. Concept: Practical over Theory

In the DigiTruck, we don't use "pen and paper" tests. We use "Performance-Based Assessment." A participant is certified when they can *perform* a task safely on their own device.

  • Observable Output: Can they scan a QR? Can they find a gov receipt?
  • Confidence Score: Does the participant *feel* they can repeat the task without help?
  • Safety Gate: Any certification requires a 100% score on the CORE-03 Safety rules.

🛠 Lab Activity: Conducting a Handoff Assessment

Practice the "Certification Check" with a peer:

  • Step 1: Ask the participant to show you their "Ministerial Reference Number" from the S4 Lab.
  • Step 2: Ask them to identify one "Red Flag" in a sample scam message.
  • Step 3: Observe them opening their banking app and identifying where the "Freeze Card" button is.

🛑 Certification Ethics

A certificate is a "Badge of Trust." Never issue a certificate to someone who cannot demonstrate the safety protocols. If they are struggling, refer them to the **GN Micro-Sessions** for more practice rather than passing them prematurely.

2. Digital Badge Issuance

Managing the digital records of your participants.

  • Data Entry: Recording successful completions in the GDEC registry.
  • Issuance: Sending the digital certificate via WhatsApp or Email.
  • Celebration: The "Capstone" moment at the end of Day 3 to build community pride.

📄 Trainer Checklist: Assessment Day

  1. Is the GDEC registry open and ready?
  2. Do you have the digital certificate templates ready for distribution?
  3. Have you identified participants who need "Extra Support" during the continuity phase?
