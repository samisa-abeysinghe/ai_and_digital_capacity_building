TOT-03: Safeguarding & Incident Escalation

Trainer responsibility. How to protect participants from harm and what to do when an incident occurs.

Maturity Target: M3 Applied Outcomes Focus: Protection & Ethics

1. Concept: Protecting the Vulnerable

Digital empowerment can also expose participants to risks. Our "Safeguarding" protocol ensures that the DigiTruck is a safe space for everyone, especially women and children.

  • Data Privacy: Never collect or photograph participant NICs or passwords for your own records.
  • Safe Environment: Ensure participants know they can ask questions without judgment.
  • Digital Footprint: Ensure all dummy apps are cleared of participant data after each session.

🚑 Incident Escalation Protocol

If a participant reports a digital scam, harassment, or a lost account, follow these steps:

  • Step 1 (Stop): Do not attempt to "fix" the legal or financial issue yourself.
  • Step 2 (Report): Document the time, date, and nature of the incident (anonymously).
  • Step 3 (Escalate): Inform the GN Cell Convenor immediately.
  • Step 4 (Advise): Provide the official hotline for the bank or the National Cyber Security Center.

2. Working with the GN Cell

The GN Digital Empowerment Cell (GDEC) is your partner in safety.

  • Official Presence: Always ensure at least one GN official is nearby during sessions.
  • Community Trust: Use the GN Cell to verify the "dedicated crowd" before the truck arrives.

📄 Job Aid: Daily Security Check

  1. Are all participant sessions logged out?
  2. Have you cleared the cache on shared DigiTruck tablets?
  3. Did you remind participants one last time about the "Never Share OTP" rule?
