TOT-01: Facilitation & Pacing

Train-the-Trainer (ToT). Master the art of delivering high-impact immersion sessions for diverse rural groups.

Maturity Target: M3 Applied Outcomes Focus: Adult Learning & Facilitation

1. Concept: Respectful Facilitation

As a trainer, your goal is to empower, not just lecture. In rural settings, the "Mature yet Simple" rule applies to your teaching style as much as the content.

  • Horizontal Learning: Treat participants as peers with life experience.
  • No Technical Jargon: Replace "interoperability" with "everything working together."
  • Confidence First: The goal is to remove the "fear of the machine."

🛠 Lab Activity: Managing the Room

Practice these three facilitation techniques:

  • Technique 1: The "Check-in" Loop. Every 15 minutes, pause to ensure no one is left behind on a step.
  • Technique 2: Peer Mentoring. If one participant is faster, ask them to help the person sitting next to them.
  • Technique 3: The "Wait Time." After asking a question, wait 10 seconds before giving the answer to allow rural users to process it in their own context.

🛑 Pacing Warning

The DigiTruck day is intense (7 sessions). Do not rush. If the group is struggling with CORE-03 (Safety), do not move to CORE-04 (Gov Services) until the "Verify Before Trust" rule is understood. Safety is the gate.

📄 Trainer Checklist: Session Prep

  1. Are all devices charged and connected to the DigiTruck hotspot?
  2. Do you have enough "Print-Ready" cheat sheets for everyone?
  3. Have you coordinated with the GN Cell for the handoff plan?
  4. Is the "Simulator" screen ready for the lab activities?
